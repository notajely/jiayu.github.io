About Me

I am currently a Master student in Digital Humanities at Uppsala University, focusing on Computational Linguistics and Cognitive Linguistics. I hold a Bachelor's degree in English Linguistics and Literature. My research primarily revolves around Computational Linguistics, NLP, and Cognitive Linguistics, particularly at the intersection of AI and Cognitive Linguistics, exploring the intricate mechanisms behind human language cognition.

Research Interests

  • AI and Human Cognition: Exploring how AI can simulate and extend human cognition through language models, particularly focusing on cultural biases and cognitive biases.
  • Computational Linguistics & Cross-Cultural Semantics: Investigating the effects of data inequality on language models’ knowledge representation across different cultural contexts.
  • Neuro-linguistics & Cognitive Linguistics Applications: Analyzing language models’ ability to represent human cognitive structures, especially in multilingual contexts.
  • AI Bias and Fairness: Researching how AI biases reflect or perpetuate human cognitive biases, with a focus on improving model fairness.
  • AI-Generated Cognition & Social Biases: Studying the extent to which AI models develop human-like social biases, even without explicit human input.

Research covers cognitive biases in LLMs in multilingual settings, using corpus methods to analyze cultural/cognitive differences in AI texts, focusing on model fairness and cross-cultural semantics.
